Big Questions layout — stacked rows vs. card grid

Anthropic's anthropic.com/research uses a 2-col card grid with category tags for their research topics; your current layout is full-width stacked rows with a figure on each. Tradeoff: rows = full paragraph reading; grid = journalist scan density. Both shown below using Q1-Q4 text.

A · current (stacked rows)

Low Vitamin D: Cause or Effect?

Low vitamin D is associated with many diseases. Medicine assumed low vitamin D was causative. We asked whether the opposite was true: does disease cause low vitamin D? In asthma, obesity, and aging, the answer was yes.

MR scatter figure

Is the Dose the Drug?

Most trials give pharmacologic doses to people who aren't deficient. That's not the same biology as correcting a deficiency. The dose isn't just a number — it changes what the molecule does.

O2 flux figure
B · grid (Anthropic-style)

Phenotype

Low Vitamin D: Cause or Effect?

Medicine assumed low vitamin D was causative. We asked whether disease causes low vitamin D. In asthma, obesity, and aging: yes.

Phenotype

How Does Disease Lower Vitamin D?

Obesity and aging directly suppress the liver enzyme that activates vitamin D. If biology varies by disease, does it vary by person?

Phenotype

Same Dose, Different Results

Two patients take the same dose. One responds. One doesn't. Genetic variation in CYP2R1 shapes who benefits.

Mechanism

Is the Dose the Drug?

High-dose vitamin D activates pathways that replacement doses don't touch. The dose changes what the molecule does.
