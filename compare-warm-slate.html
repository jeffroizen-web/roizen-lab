Research

Big Questions

Each project in our lab is driven by a fundamental unanswered question about vitamin D with clear clinical relevance.

When is low vitamin D a cause of disease vs. an effect of disease?

Our work on asthma, aging, and obesity reveals that low vitamin D can be both — and that untangling cause from consequence is essential to knowing when supplementation will actually help.

What are the mechanisms whereby diseases lower vitamin D?

We discovered that obesity and aging decrease hepatic 25-hydroxylase activity, providing mechanistic explanations for why sick patients so often have low vitamin D levels.

What underlies the differences in how different individuals or groups respond to vitamin D supplementation?

Our work on CYP2R1 coding SNPs shows that genetic variation in vitamin D metabolism shapes who benefits from supplementation — and who doesn't.

Does high-dose vitamin D have different effects than normalizing vitamin D?

Most clinical trials give pharmacologic doses to people who aren't deficient. We study whether the biology of high-dose vitamin D is fundamentally different from simply correcting a deficiency.

How does high-dose vitamin D prevent progression from prediabetes to diabetes?

Large trials show that high-dose vitamin D reduces diabetes risk in prediabetic patients. We are working to understand the mechanism — and whether it extends to other metabolic diseases.

How are the set points for body composition determined?

We discovered that vitamin D reallocates surplus calories from fat to muscle and linear growth. Now we're asking: what controls the set point that decides where those calories go?

If PTH is the right marker for vitamin D’s calcium effects, what is the marker for vitamin D’s metabolic effects?

Parathyroid hormone tells us when vitamin D is adequate for bone health. But we have no equivalent biomarker for vitamin D's effects on metabolism, immunity, or muscle. Finding one would transform clinical practice.

New preprint: High-dose vitamin D reallocates surplus calories from fat to muscle
