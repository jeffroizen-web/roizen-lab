Vitamin D does far more than build bones.

We study the non-calcium benefits of vitamin D — uncovering how it reshapes metabolism, modulates immunity, influences cancer, and redefines how we think about a simple vitamin.

Research

Big Questions

The questions driving our work at the intersection of vitamin D biology, metabolism, and human health.

01

How does vitamin D redirect calories from fat to muscle?

We discovered that high-dose vitamin D reallocates surplus calories to muscle and linear growth instead of fat, through modulation of myostatin and leptin signaling.

02

Why do obese individuals have lower vitamin D levels?

Our work revealed that obesity decreases hepatic 25-hydroxylase activity, providing a mechanistic explanation for this well-documented clinical association.

03

Can vitamin D modulate tumor biology?

We are investigating how dietary vitamin D influences tumor engraftment through regulation of vitamin D binding protein (GC protein) abundance.

04

How do genetics shape individual vitamin D needs?

Through transethnic genomic studies, we've identified population-specific variants that influence vitamin D metabolism, pointing toward precision medicine approaches.

05

Does vitamin D status during development shape lifelong health?

We study how vitamin D levels in children affect growth, immune development, and susceptibility to autoimmune conditions like type 1 diabetes.

06

What is the right dose of vitamin D — and for whom?

We're working to move beyond one-size-fits-all supplementation toward individualized therapy based on genetics and metabolic context.
