## I. PEER-REVIEWED ORIGINAL RESEARCH PUBLICATIONS

### Theme A: Vitamin D Metabolism, CYP2R1, and CYP3A4

*Core lab program -- non-calcium vitamin D biology, 25-hydroxylation, population genetics*

1. **Roizen JD\***, Hysinger EB\*, Mentch FD, Vazquez L, Connolly JJ, Bradfield JP, Almoguera B, Sleiman PM, Allen JL, Levine MA, Hakonarson H. Mendelian randomization analysis demonstrates that low vitamin D is unlikely causative for pediatric asthma. *J Allergy Clin Immunol.* 2016 Dec;138(6):1747-1749.e4. PMCID: PMC5526345. \*co-first authors
   - **Source:** CV (2017), all biosketches (2017-2025)
   - **Significance:** Used Mendelian randomization to show asthma causes low vitamin D (not vice versa). Landmark finding reversing the presumed causal direction.

2. **Roizen JD**, Casella A, Lai M, Long C, Tara Z, Caplan I, O'Lear L, Levine MA. Decreased Serum 25-Hydroxyvitamin D in Aging Male Mice Is Associated With Reduced Hepatic Cyp2r1 Abundance. *Endocrinology.* 2018 Aug 1;159(8):3083-3089. doi: 10.1210/en.2017-03028.
   - **Source:** Biosketches (2018-2025)
   - **Significance:** Showed aging decreases CYP2R1 in liver, explaining why older individuals have lower vitamin D levels.

3. **Roizen JD**, Long C, Casella A, O'Lear L, Caplan I, Lai M, Sasson I, Singh R, Makowski AJ, Simmons R, Levine MA. Obesity Decreases Hepatic 25-Hydroxylase Activity Causing Low Serum 25-Hydroxyvitamin D. *J Bone Miner Res.* 2019 Jun;34(6):1068-1073. doi: 10.1002/jbmr.3686.
   - **Source:** CV (2017 as pending), all biosketches (2018-2025)
   - **Significance:** Demonstrated obesity itself causes low vitamin D by decreasing hepatic 25-hydroxylation -- not the other way around.

4. Casella A, Long C, Zhou J, Lai M, O'Lear L, Caplan I, Levine M, **Roizen JD**. Differential frequency of CYP2R1 variants across populations reveals pathway selection for vitamin D homeostasis. *J Clin Endocrinol Metab.* 2020 Mar.
   - **Source:** Biosketches (2020-2025)
   - **Significance:** Found evidence of evolutionary selection for non-functional CYP2R1 variants in populations with high UV exposure. Implies African-Americans may need more vitamin D supplementation.

5. **Roizen JD**, Li D, O'Lear L, Javaid MK, Shaw NJ, Ebeling PR, Nguyen HH, Rodda CP, Thummel KE, Thacher TD, Hakonarson H, Levine MA. CYP2R1 Mutations Impair Generation of 25-hydroxyvitamin D and Cause an Atypical Form of Vitamin D Deficiency. *J Clin Endocrinol Metab.* 2015 Jul;100(7):E1005-13. PMCID: PMC4490307.
   - **Source:** CV (2017) -- listed as Thacher TD, Fischer PR, Singh RJ, Roizen J, Levine MA (note: Roizen is middle author on this one; the CV lists it as Thacher first author)
   - **Note:** The CV entry (item 9) lists Thacher as first author. Cross-reference with PubMed for correct author order.

6. **Roizen JD**, Li D, O'Lear L, Javaid MK, Shaw NJ, Ebeling PR, Nguyen HH, Rodda CP, Thummel KE, Thacher TD, Hakonarson H, and Levine MA. A Recurrent CYP3A4 Mutation As a Cause of Rickets. *J Clin Invest.* 2018 May 1;128(5):1913-1918. PMCID: PMC5919884.
   - **Source:** CV (2017 as pending/in press), all biosketches (2017-2025)
   - **Significance:** Identified a novel CYP3A4 gain-of-function mutation causing vitamin D-dependent rickets type 3. First demonstration that CYP3A4 plays a clinically relevant role in vitamin D metabolism.

### Theme B: Energy Balance, Hypothalamic Obesity, and Oxytocin Neurons

*Central control of energy expenditure via PVN neurons*

7. **Roizen J\***, Xi D\*, Lai M, Gandhi N, Kublaoui B. Paraventricular nucleus Sim1 neuron ablation mediated obesity is resistant to high fat diet. *PLoS One.* 2013;8(11):e81087. PMCID: PMC3834298. \*co-first authors
   - **Source:** CV (2017), all biosketches (2017-2025)
   - **Significance:** PVN-Sim1 neuron ablated mice paradoxically lost weight on high-fat diet. Points to potential therapy for hypothalamic obesity.

8. Xi D, Long C, Lai M, Casella A, O'Lear L, Kublaoui B, **Roizen JD**. Ablation of Oxytocin Neurons Causes a Deficit in Cold Stress Response. *J Endocr Soc.* 2017 Jul 12;1(8):1041-1055. PMCID: PMC5686635.
   - **Source:** CV (2017 as in press), all biosketches (2017-2025)
   - **Significance:** Mice lacking oxytocin neurons gain weight in cold, with decreased brown fat activation.

### Theme C: Clinical Endocrinology (PHP1A, Calcium, Hyperparathyroidism)

*Clinical/translational work in pediatric endocrine disease*

9. **Roizen JD\***, Danzig J, Groleau V, McCormack S, Casella A, Harrington J, Sochett E, Tershakovec A, Zemel BS, Stallings VA, Levine MA. Resting Energy Expenditure Is Decreased in Pseudohypoparathyroidism Type 1A. *J Clin Endocrinol Metab.* 2016 Mar;101(3):880-8. PMCID: PMC4803160.
   - **Source:** CV (2017), all biosketches (2017-2025)
   - **Significance:** First demonstration that PHP1A causes decreased energy expenditure (not just increased intake) as a cause of obesity. Unique among all described human obesities.

10. **Roizen JD**, Shah V, Levine MA, Carlow DC. Determination of reference intervals for serum total calcium in the vitamin D-replete pediatric population. *J Clin Endocrinol Metab.* 2013 Dec;98(12):E1946-50. PMCID: PMC3849669.
    - **Source:** CV (2017)

11. **Roizen J**, Levine MA. A meta-analysis comparing the biochemistry of primary hyperparathyroidism in youths to the biochemistry of primary hyperparathyroidism in adults. *J Clin Endocrinol Metab.* 2014 Dec;99(12):4555-64. PMCID: PMC4255125.
    - **Source:** CV (2017)

### Theme D: Reproductive Physiology (PhD Thesis Work)

*Oxytocin, prostaglandins, and labor timing*

12. **Roizen J**, Luedke CE, Herzog ED, Muglia LJ. Oxytocin in the circadian timing of birth. *PLoS One.* 2007 Sep 26;2(9):e922. PMCID: PMC1976559.
    - **Source:** CV (2017), biosketches (2017-2025)
    - **Significance:** First demonstration that a non-clock protein (oxytocin) is required for circadian distribution of labor.

13. **Roizen JD**, Asada M, Tong M, Tai HH, Muglia LJ. Preterm birth without progesterone withdrawal in 15-hydroxyprostaglandin dehydrogenase hypomorphic mice. *Mol Endocrinol.* 2008 Jan;22(1):105-12. PMCID: PMC2194629.
    - **Source:** CV (2017), biosketches (2017-2025)
    - **Significance:** Generated first mouse model of preterm labor. These mice labor without progesterone decrease -- a uniquely humanized model.

14. **Roizen JD**, Asada M, Tong M, Tai HH, Muglia LJ. Early pregnancy loss in 15-hydroxyprostaglandin dehydrogenase knockout (15-HPGD-/-) mice due to requirement for embryo 15-HPGD activity. *Sci Rep.* 2019 Nov.
    - **Source:** Biosketches (2019-2025)
    - **Significance:** Revealed a novel essential role for prostaglandin breakdown in implantation.
