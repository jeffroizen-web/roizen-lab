Rethinking Vitamin D

The Roizen Lab explores what vitamin D does beyond calcium — how it reshapes body composition, modulates cancer biology, and why the right dose depends on who you are.

Research

Big Questions

Each project in our lab is anchored by a question about vitamin D that challenges conventional thinking.

⚖

How does vitamin D redirect calories from fat to muscle?

High-dose vitamin D reallocates surplus calories to muscle and growth through myostatin and leptin signaling.

⚛

Why do obese individuals have lower vitamin D?

Obesity decreases hepatic 25-hydroxylase activity, explaining the link between body fat and vitamin D deficiency.

☢

Can vitamin D modulate tumor biology?

Dietary vitamin D influences tumor engraftment through regulation of vitamin D binding protein abundance.

⚒

How do genetics shape vitamin D needs?

Population-specific genetic variants influence vitamin D metabolism, pointing toward precision supplementation.

☘

Does early vitamin D status shape lifelong health?

Vitamin D levels in children affect growth, immunity, and susceptibility to autoimmune conditions.

⌖

What is the right dose — and for whom?

Moving beyond one-size-fits-all toward individualized vitamin D therapy based on genetics and metabolism.
