What can Vitamin D do beyond calcium?

The Roizen Lab investigates the diverse, non-classical biological roles of vitamin D — from muscle metabolism and immune regulation to growth and cancer biology.

Big Questions

Our research is driven by fundamental questions about vitamin D's roles beyond calcium homeostasis.

How does vitamin D redirect calories from fat to muscle?

We discovered that high-dose vitamin D reallocates surplus calories to muscle and linear growth instead of fat, through modulation of myostatin and leptin signaling.

Why do obese individuals have lower vitamin D levels?

Our work revealed that obesity decreases hepatic 25-hydroxylase activity, providing a mechanistic explanation for the well-documented association between obesity and vitamin D deficiency.

Can vitamin D modulate tumor biology?

We are investigating how dietary vitamin D influences tumor engraftment through regulation of vitamin D binding protein (GC protein) abundance.

How do genetics shape individual vitamin D needs?

Through transethnic genomic studies, we've identified population-specific genetic variants that influence vitamin D metabolism, pointing toward precision medicine approaches.

Does vitamin D status during development shape lifelong health?

We study how vitamin D levels in children affect growth, immune development, and susceptibility to autoimmune conditions like type 1 diabetes.

What is the right dose of vitamin D — and for whom?

We are working to move beyond one-size-fits-all supplementation toward individualized vitamin D therapy based on genetics and metabolic context.
